# 3D Image Projections

These are the exact prompt combinations dynamically generated by the 3D Camera tool depending on the selected source and target points.

## Base Template
Use image 1 and create another projection of the exact same space.
Only the camera angle and position changes.
[BACK VIEW CONDITION]
[CAMERA ACTION CONDITION]
[ZOOM CONDITION]
[USER NOTE HERE]
The image's objects, positions, architecture, and characters must remain exactly the same as reference image 1.
Only the camera perspective changes.

## Conditions

### 1. Back View Condition
**Trigger**: When the target point is on the BACK grid (red points).
**Text**:
> Camera turns 180 degrees to reveal the back (opposite) view of the space not visible on this image and that should be consistent with the style, architecture and light of the original space.

### 2. Zoom Condition
**Trigger**: When the zoom level is modified.
**Zoom Out Text (e.g. 50%)**:
> The camera has pulled back, showing 200% more of the environment.
**Zoom In Text (e.g. 150%)**:
> The camera has zoomed in closely on the subject at 150% scale.

### 3. Camera Action Combinations
These combine the `srcPos` (where the camera is) with the `lookTowardsStr` (where it looks) and the `revealVertical` (what it reveals).

**Example 1: Center Front -> Right Front**
> Camera is now positioned on the CENTER looking towards the RIGHT FRONT SIDE of the space, revealing the right front side of the space.

**Example 2: Left Upper -> Center Down (Back Grid)**
> Camera is now positioned on the LEFT SIDE looking towards the CENTER DOWN SIDE of the space, revealing the center down side (topdown view) of the back (opposite side of the image).

**Example 3: Bottom Right -> Top Left**
> Camera is now positioned on the RIGHT SIDE looking towards the LEFT UP SIDE of the space, revealing the left upper side (low angle view tilting up) of the space.

**Example 4: Top Center -> Bottom Center**
> Camera is now positioned on the CENTER looking towards the CENTER DOWN SIDE of the space, revealing the center down side (topdown view) of the space.
